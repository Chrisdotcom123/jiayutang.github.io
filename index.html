About

Who I Am

I am currently a Master's student in Food Science and Technology at the University of Nebraska–Lincoln, working with Dr. Edward Deehan. I earned dual Bachelor's degrees from Northwest A&F University (NWAFU) and the University of Nebraska–Lincoln (UNL) through a 3+1 dual-degree program.

My current research focuses on a clinical trial — plus a systematic review — exploring the effects of different types of beans on human health and gut microbiome. Beyond research, I am also drawn to philosophy, photography, and cooking.

Research

Research Experience

Graduate Research Assistant

Department of Food Science and Technology · University of Nebraska–Lincoln

Advisor: Dr. Edward Deehan

Jul 2024 – Present

Human Clinical Trials

  • Co-developed NIH-style study protocol, informed consent form, and recruitment materials; authored 4 Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) and 2 clinical trial workflows for the Deehan Lab.
  • Coordinating a 12-week randomized crossover clinical trial investigating dry bean market-class–specific effects on gut microbiota and cardiometabolic health markers (IRB Approved; ClinicalTrials.gov Registered).
  • Coordinating a statewide human biobank study to collect and store blood and stool samples from volunteers (IRB Approved).
  • Led 200+ clinic visits; managed participant recruitment, scheduling, documentation, and study supplies.
  • Collected and processed stool samples, blood samples, 24-hr blood pressure data, and dietary information; applied statistical analysis using R.
  • Conducted DNA extraction from fecal samples for qPCR analysis; analyzed stool pH, moisture, and short-chain fatty acid content.

Systematic Review

  • Conducting a systematic review to identify effects of distinct dietary pulse market classes on gut microbiome and surrogate health outcomes in humans.
  • Developed a PICOS-based research proposal; applied machine learning tools (ASReview, TERA-Tool) to screen 1,800+ citations.
  • Applied hybrid human-AI (LLM; Google Gemini) approach for preliminary data extraction, independently validating model performance against human reviewers.
